# Choosing the Right High School

## Why It Matters
If your family has a choice of high schools (public, magnet, charter, private, or specialty), this decision can significantly impact your child's academic trajectory, college options, and overall experience.

## Factors to Consider

### Academic Quality
- What AP/IB/Honors courses are offered?
- What are the graduation rates and college acceptance rates?
- What is the student-to-teacher ratio?
- Are there academic support services (tutoring, study centers)?
- What standardized test scores does the school achieve?

### Extracurricular Opportunities
- What clubs, sports, and activities are available?
- Are there opportunities aligned with your child's interests?
- How competitive are the programs?
- Are there community service or volunteer programs?

### College Counseling
- How many students per counselor?
- Does the school have dedicated college counseling?
- What percentage of students attend 4-year colleges?
- Does the school have relationships with colleges?

### Environment & Culture
- Does your child feel safe and welcome during visits?
- What is the school's approach to discipline?
- How diverse is the student body?
- What is the school's culture around academics vs. athletics vs. arts?

### Practical Considerations
- Location and commute time
- Cost (for private schools)
- Before/after school care or activities
- Transportation options

## Types of High Schools
- **Zoned public:** Your default neighborhood school — free, conveniently located
- **Magnet schools:** Specialized focus (STEM, arts, IB) — may require application
- **Charter schools:** Publicly funded, independently operated — lottery admission
- **Private schools:** Tuition-based, may offer financial aid — application required
- **Homeschool/Online:** Flexible scheduling, self-paced — requires family commitment

## How to Evaluate
1. Visit during a regular school day (not open house — see the real environment)
2. Talk to current students and parents
3. Review the course catalog — does it offer what your child needs?
4. Ask about college counseling specifically
5. Consider your child's personality and learning style
6. Trust your gut — does the school feel like the right fit?

# How Parents Can Support — Grade by Grade

## Your Role
Your job isn't to do the work for your child — it's to create the environment, provide the tools, and offer the encouragement that helps them succeed on their own. As they grow, your role shifts from manager to coach to consultant.

## 6th Grade — Be the Organizer
- Help set up organizational systems (planner, binder, study space)
- Establish homework routines and screen time boundaries
- Attend school events and meet teachers
- Encourage trying new activities without pressure to commit
- Model reading and learning at home

## 7th Grade — Be the Encourager
- Shift from directing to guiding — ask "What's your plan?" instead of "Do your homework"
- Help them research interests and activities
- Start conversations about what they enjoy and why
- Monitor grades but let them take ownership of talking to teachers
- Discuss the concept of high school preparation

## 8th Grade — Be the Planner
- Attend high school orientation together
- Help with high school course selection — provide input, let them decide
- Discuss high school expectations openly
- Start exploring potential high schools if you have options
- Begin introducing the concept of college (casually, without pressure)

## 9th Grade — Be the Coach
- Help create a 4-year course plan
- Support extracurricular choices (depth over breadth)
- Discuss GPA and why freshman year matters
- Visit a college campus "just to see" — normalize the idea
- Start a college savings conversation (even if the amount is small)

## 10th Grade — Be the Strategist
- Support PSAT preparation
- Help research AP/honors course decisions
- Begin building a college list together
- Attend college fairs as a family
- Discuss financial realities of college openly and honestly
- Help them build a resume of activities and accomplishments

## 11th Grade — Be the Navigator
- Support SAT/ACT preparation (consider a prep course or tutor)
- Visit colleges together (in person when possible)
- Help finalize the college list (reach, match, safety)
- Proofread essays but don't write them
- Ensure recommendation letters are requested on time
- Complete FAFSA preparation (gather financial documents)
- Manage your own anxiety — your stress transfers to your child

## 12th Grade — Be the Anchor
- Help manage application deadlines (but let them own the process)
- Complete FAFSA and CSS Profile together (October 1 deadline)
- Support them through rejections AND acceptances
- Compare financial aid packages objectively
- Help with the final decision — but let it be THEIR decision
- Prepare for the transition — emotionally and practically

## General Tips for Every Grade
- **Ask open-ended questions** — "What was interesting today?" not "How was school?"
- **Praise effort, not just results** — "I'm proud of how hard you studied" matters
- **Let them fail safely** — Small failures now prevent bigger ones later
- **Stay informed but not overbearing** — Know what's happening without hovering
- **Take care of yourself** — A stressed parent creates a stressed student

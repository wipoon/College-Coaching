# College Admissions Glossary

## A-C
- **ACT** — American College Testing; standardized test accepted by all US colleges
- **AP (Advanced Placement)** — College-level courses taken in high school; exams can earn college credit
- **Candidate Reply Date** — May 1; deadline to commit to a college after acceptance
- **Class Rank** — Your academic standing compared to classmates based on GPA
- **College Board** — Organization that administers the SAT, AP exams, and CSS Profile
- **Common App** — Application platform accepted by 1,000+ colleges
- **CSS Profile** — Financial aid form required by some private colleges (in addition to FAFSA)
- **Coalition App** — Alternative application platform accepted by 150+ colleges

## D-F
- **Deferral** — When a college moves your Early application to the Regular Decision pool
- **Demonstrated Interest** — Showing a college you're genuinely interested (visits, emails, info sessions)
- **Early Action (EA)** — Apply early (Nov) with non-binding early response
- **Early Decision (ED)** — Apply early (Nov) with binding commitment if accepted
- **EFC (Expected Family Contribution)** — Now called SAI; what your family is expected to pay
- **FAFSA** — Free Application for Federal Student Aid; required for federal financial aid
- **Fee Waiver** — Allows students to apply without paying application fees (based on need)
- **Financial Aid Award Letter** — Document showing how much aid a college offers you

## G-L
- **Gap Year** — Taking a year off between high school and college (can be very valuable if planned well)
- **GPA (Grade Point Average)** — Numeric average of your grades (usually on a 4.0 scale)
- **Holistic Review** — Considering all aspects of a student, not just grades and scores
- **Honors Course** — Advanced version of a standard class (above regular, below AP)
- **IB (International Baccalaureate)** — Rigorous international curriculum; similar to AP
- **Legacy** — Having a parent or grandparent who attended the same college
- **Liberal Arts** — Broad education across multiple disciplines (not just "arts")

## M-R
- **Major** — Your primary field of study in college
- **Merit Aid** — Scholarships based on academic, athletic, or other achievements (not financial need)
- **Minor** — Secondary field of study (less coursework than a major)
- **Need-Based Aid** — Financial aid based on your family's ability to pay
- **Need-Blind Admission** — College doesn't consider your financial need when deciding admission
- **Net Price** — Actual cost after all grants and scholarships (sticker price minus free money)
- **Net Price Calculator** — Tool on every college website to estimate your actual cost
- **PSAT/NMSQT** — Practice SAT that also qualifies students for National Merit Scholarships
- **Reach School** — College where acceptance is a stretch for your academic profile
- **Regular Decision** — Standard application deadline (typically Jan 1-15)
- **Rolling Admission** — Applications reviewed as they arrive (no fixed deadline)

## S-Z
- **SAI (Student Aid Index)** — Replaced EFC; determines financial aid eligibility from FAFSA
- **Safety School** — College where you're very likely to be accepted (and would happily attend)
- **SAT** — Scholastic Assessment Test; standardized test accepted by all US colleges
- **Sticker Price** — Published total cost before any financial aid
- **Supplemental Essay** — Additional essay required by specific colleges
- **Target/Match School** — College where your profile is a strong fit
- **Test-Optional** — College doesn't require SAT/ACT scores for admission
- **Transcript** — Official record of your high school courses and grades
- **Waitlist** — Neither accepted nor rejected; may receive an offer if spots open up
- **Yield** — Percentage of accepted students who choose to enroll

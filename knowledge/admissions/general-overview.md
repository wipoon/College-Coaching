# How College Admissions Works

## The Big Picture
College admissions is how colleges decide which students to accept. Every college has its own process, but most look at a combination of the same factors. Understanding this early helps you prepare — not stress — over many years.

## What Colleges Look At

### 1. Academic Record (Most Important)
- **GPA** — Your grade point average across all classes
- **Course rigor** — Did you challenge yourself with honors, AP, or IB courses?
- **Grade trends** — Improving grades show growth; declining grades are a concern
- **Class rank** — Where you stand relative to your classmates (not all schools rank)

### 2. Standardized Tests
- **SAT or ACT** — Most colleges accept either (some are now test-optional)
- **AP Exams** — Can earn college credit and show subject mastery
- **PSAT** — Practice for SAT; qualifies you for National Merit Scholarships (10th/11th grade)

### 3. Extracurricular Activities
- Quality over quantity — depth and leadership matter more than a long list
- Colleges want to see commitment, growth, and impact
- Activities should reflect your genuine interests

### 4. Essays
- **Personal statement** — Your chance to show who you are beyond grades
- **Supplemental essays** — School-specific; show why you're a good fit
- Authenticity and strong writing matter most

### 5. Recommendation Letters
- Usually 2-3 from teachers and your school counselor
- Build genuine relationships with teachers starting in 9th grade
- Ask teachers who know you well, not just the ones who gave you an A

### 6. Interview (Not All Schools)
- Some colleges offer optional or required interviews
- Practice talking about yourself, your interests, and why you want to attend

## Application Types
| Type | When | Binding? | Notes |
|------|------|----------|-------|
| Early Decision (ED) | Nov 1-15 | Yes — must attend if accepted | Apply to only ONE school ED |
| Early Action (EA) | Nov 1-15 | No — can still compare offers | Can apply EA to multiple schools |
| Regular Decision (RD) | Jan 1-15 | No | Most common; compare all offers |
| Rolling Admission | Anytime | No | Schools review as applications arrive |

## The Timeline (11th-12th Grade)
- **11th grade fall:** Research colleges, visit campuses
- **11th grade spring:** Take SAT/ACT, ask for recommendation letters
- **Summer before 12th:** Write essays, finalize college list
- **12th grade fall:** Submit applications (ED/EA by Nov, RD by Jan)
- **12th grade spring:** Receive decisions, compare offers, commit by May 1

## Important Terms
- **Reach school** — You might get in, but it's competitive for your profile
- **Match school** — Your stats are in range; good chance of acceptance
- **Safety school** — You're very likely to be accepted; still a school you'd happily attend
- **Yield** — The percentage of accepted students who actually enroll
- **Holistic review** — Considering the whole student, not just numbers

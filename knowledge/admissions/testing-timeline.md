# Testing Timeline

## Overview
Standardized tests are ONE part of your college application. Many schools are now test-optional, but strong scores can still help — especially for merit scholarships.

## Test-by-Test Guide

### PSAT/NMSQT
- **What:** Practice SAT; qualifies for National Merit Scholarships
- **When:** October of 10th and 11th grade
- **Cost:** ~$20 (often paid by school)
- **Why take it:** Practice for SAT, scholarship qualification, identify strengths/weaknesses
- **Prep:** Take a practice test, review math and reading fundamentals

### SAT
- **What:** College admissions test (reading, writing, math)
- **Score range:** 400-1600
- **When:** Offered 7 times/year (March, May, June, August, October, November, December)
- **Best time to take:** Spring of 11th grade (retake fall of 12th if needed)
- **Cost:** ~$60 (fee waivers available)
- **Prep timeline:** Start 3-6 months before test date

### ACT
- **What:** Alternative to SAT (English, math, reading, science, optional writing)
- **Score range:** 1-36
- **When:** Offered 7 times/year (February, April, June, July, September, October, December)
- **Best time to take:** Spring of 11th grade
- **Cost:** ~$65-$93 (fee waivers available)
- **Note:** Some students do better on ACT than SAT — try both with practice tests

### AP Exams
- **What:** Subject-specific exams that can earn college credit
- **Score range:** 1-5 (3+ usually earns credit)
- **When:** May of each year (during AP exam period)
- **Cost:** ~$98 per exam (fee reductions available)
- **Why take them:** College credit, demonstrate mastery, strengthen applications

## Recommended Testing Timeline

| Grade | What to Do |
|-------|-----------|
| 9th | Focus on academics; no standardized tests needed yet |
| 10th (Oct) | Take PSAT for practice — don't stress about the score |
| 10th (Spring) | Take a full-length SAT AND ACT practice test to see which suits you better |
| 11th (Oct) | Take PSAT/NMSQT (this one counts for National Merit!) |
| 11th (Mar-Jun) | Take SAT or ACT (whichever you prefer based on practice) |
| 11th (May) | Take AP exams for any AP courses |
| 12th (Aug-Oct) | Retake SAT/ACT if you want to improve your score |
| 12th (May) | Take AP exams for senior year AP courses |

## SAT vs. ACT — Which is Better for You?

| Factor | SAT | ACT |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Sections | Reading, Writing, Math | English, Math, Reading, Science |
| Time pressure | More time per question | Less time per question |
| Math focus | Algebra, data analysis, some advanced math | Broader math including geometry, trig |
| Science | No dedicated section | Has a science reasoning section |
| Good for | Strong readers, careful workers | Fast workers, science-oriented students |

**Tip:** Take a full practice test of BOTH and see which score is stronger relative to each test's scale. Most colleges accept either — they don't prefer one over the other.

## Test-Optional Schools
Many colleges no longer require SAT/ACT scores. However:
- Submitting strong scores can still help your application
- Merit scholarships often still require test scores
- Check each college's current testing policy on their admissions website

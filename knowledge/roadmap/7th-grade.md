# 7th Grade — Growth Year: Strengthen & Explore

> **What you should know:** 7th grade is where you shift from "figuring things out" to "getting good at things." You've survived the transition to middle school — now it's time to go deeper. This year is about strengthening the habits you started in 6th grade, exploring your interests with more intention, and starting to think about who you're becoming. The choices you make now start to shape your path.

## 🎓 Academics

### Fall Semester
- Take your GPA seriously — understand that strong grades now build momentum for high school
- Talk to your counselor about honors or advanced classes available for 8th grade
- Focus on writing skills — practice the 5-paragraph essay structure until it feels natural
- Set a goal to read 20+ books this year (a mix of assigned and personal choice)
- Start each week by previewing what's due — no more "I forgot" excuses
- Ask teachers for specific feedback on assignments, not just "what did I get?"

### Spring Semester
- Identify subjects where you could challenge yourself with harder material
- Practice writing in different formats: persuasive essays, research summaries, personal narratives
- Seek out extra credit opportunities — not because you need them, but to stretch yourself
- Form a study group with classmates who take school seriously
- Request placement in honors or advanced classes for 8th grade if you're ready
- Finish the year with a GPA you're proud of — no coasting in the final weeks

### Skills to Master
- Write a clear 5-paragraph essay with an introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion
- Read and annotate a text for key themes and evidence
- Self-assess your work before turning it in — check for errors, completeness, and quality
- Maintain a B+ or higher average across all core subjects

## 🏆 Extracurriculars

### Fall Semester
- Deepen your commitment to 1-2 activities from last year — don't just show up, contribute
- Take on a small leadership role: help organize an event, mentor a younger student, lead a project
- Start a community service project or join an existing one — aim for meaningful impact
- If you haven't found your "thing" yet, try 1-2 new activities this semester

### Spring Semester
- Track your involvement — hours, roles, accomplishments (start a simple log)
- Plan and execute a community service project with measurable results (items collected, hours served, people helped)
- Explore activities outside school: community sports leagues, arts programs, faith-based groups, coding clubs
- Begin thinking about which activities you want to carry into 8th grade and high school

### Skills to Master
- Commit to an activity for a full year without quitting when it gets hard
- Take initiative within your group — volunteer for tasks without being asked
- Balance 2-3 activities with strong academic performance
- Complete a community service project from start to finish

## 🧠 Skills & Habits

### Fall Semester
- Learn the Cornell note-taking method and use it in at least 2 classes
- Create your own weekly study schedule — plan study sessions, not just homework time
- Practice active recall: close your notes and quiz yourself instead of re-reading
- Introduce spaced repetition for test prep — review material over multiple days, not just the night before
- Manage multiple classes with different due dates using a planner or digital calendar

### Spring Semester
- Refine your study schedule based on what's working and what isn't
- Practice test-taking strategies: time management, process of elimination, reading questions carefully
- Build the habit of reviewing returned tests and assignments — learn from your mistakes
- Start using a simple to-do list system to track daily and weekly tasks
- Practice the "eat the frog" technique — do your hardest task first

### Skills to Master
- Take Cornell notes independently in any class
- Create and follow a weekly study schedule without parent help
- Prepare for a test over 3+ days using active recall and spaced repetition
- Juggle homework, studying, and activities using a written system

## 🔍 College & Career Exploration

### What to Do This Year
- Research 3 careers that interest you — what education do they require? What's a typical day like?
- Learn the different types of colleges: community college, state university, private college, trade/vocational school
- Attend your school's college or career day if offered — ask questions!
- Start a "What Interests Me" journal — write down subjects, activities, and topics that excite you
- Talk to 2-3 adults about their career paths — how did they get where they are?
- Explore what "college readiness" means and what high school classes matter most

### Conversation Starters
- "What surprised you most about college?"
- "If you could go back, what would you study differently?"
- "What skills from middle school actually helped you later in life?"
- "Can you tell me about a career that uses both [subject A] and [subject B]?"

## 💪 Personal Growth

### Fall Semester
- Practice public speaking — volunteer to present in class, speak up in group discussions
- Learn healthy conflict resolution: listen first, express your perspective calmly, find compromise
- Take ownership of your daily routine — wake up, get ready, and manage your time independently
- Set 2-3 goals for the semester using the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)

### Spring Semester
- Start learning financial literacy basics: what things cost, how saving works, needs vs. wants
- Practice writing emails to teachers or other adults — learn proper greeting, body, and sign-off
- Reflect on your strengths and areas for growth — write them down honestly
- Build resilience: when something goes wrong, ask "what can I learn?" before "why me?"
- Seek feedback from trusted adults and actually apply it

### Skills to Master
- Deliver a 2-3 minute presentation in front of a class without reading from notes
- Resolve a disagreement with a friend or classmate maturely
- Send a properly formatted email to a teacher
- Create and track progress on a SMART goal independently

## ☀️ Summer Checklist
- [ ] Read 5+ books (mix of fiction, nonfiction, and at least one challenging read)
- [ ] Job shadow someone or volunteer regularly (10+ hours)
- [ ] Attend a summer enrichment program, camp, or class in an area of interest
- [ ] Start a passion project: a blog, art portfolio, coding project, garden, or small business
- [ ] Research 3 careers and write a short paragraph about each
- [ ] Set academic and personal goals for 8th grade
- [ ] Practice a skill daily for at least 2 weeks (instrument, language, coding, sport)
- [ ] Have a "career conversation" with 2 adults you admire

## ⚠️ Red Flags to Watch
- GPA dropping below a B average without a plan to improve
- Quitting every activity before giving it a real chance (less than one semester)
- No involvement in any extracurricular, club, sport, or community activity
- Relying entirely on parents for time management, homework tracking, and reminders
- Avoiding challenging classes because they seem "too hard"
- Social drama consuming all your energy and focus — no healthy coping strategies

## 🔗 How This Connects to College
Here's the truth: **7th grade is when patterns form.** The study habits, time management skills, and interests you develop this year become the foundation of your high school identity. Colleges don't just look at grades — they look for students who are curious, committed, and growing. Every book you read, every activity you stick with, and every challenge you push through is building the story you'll eventually tell on your college application. You're writing the first chapters right now.

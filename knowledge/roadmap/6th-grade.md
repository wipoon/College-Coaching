# 6th Grade — Foundation Year: Build the Basics

> **What you should know:** 6th grade is your fresh start! Whether you're entering middle school or just leveling up, this year is about building the habits and skills that will carry you all the way through high school and beyond. College feels far away — and it is! But the habits you build NOW are the foundation everything else builds on.

## 🎓 Academics

### Fall Semester
- Get organized from day one — use a planner (paper or digital) for every assignment
- Learn your school's grading system and understand how GPA works
- Attend every class and participate — raise your hand at least once per class
- Set up a consistent homework spot at home with no distractions
- Start building a relationship with each teacher — introduce yourself

### Spring Semester
- Identify your strongest and weakest subjects — get help early if you're struggling
- Aim for all A's and B's — this is the year to build your academic confidence
- Start reading for pleasure — aim for one book per month outside of class
- Learn basic study techniques: re-reading notes, flashcards, practice problems
- Finish the year strong — don't slack off in the last month

### Skills to Master
- Organize a binder or digital folder system for each class
- Write your assignments down every day without reminders
- Complete homework before screens/games
- Read independently for 20+ minutes daily

## 🏆 Extracurriculars

### Fall Semester
- Explore at least 2 clubs or activities your school offers
- Try something completely new — art, coding, debate, sports, music
- Attend school events to meet people and find what interests you
- If your school has a community service option, sign up

### Spring Semester
- Commit to at least 1 activity you enjoy — start building consistency
- Try a spring sport or activity you haven't done before
- Look into summer camps, programs, or volunteering opportunities
- Start a simple hobby outside school (drawing, building, cooking, etc.)

### Skills to Master
- Show up consistently to your activities — reliability matters
- Try things even when they feel uncomfortable
- Learn to balance schoolwork with activities

## 🧠 Skills & Habits

### Fall Semester
- Create a weekly schedule that includes homework time, activities, and free time
- Learn to use a planner or calendar app — write everything down
- Practice the "2-minute rule": if something takes less than 2 minutes, do it now
- Start each study session by reviewing what you learned that day

### Spring Semester
- Build a nightly routine: pack your bag, review tomorrow's schedule, get enough sleep
- Learn basic note-taking — write key points, not every word
- Practice breaking big assignments into smaller steps
- Develop a "study ritual" — same time, same place, same routine

### Skills to Master
- Manage your time without a parent reminding you
- Take notes in class without being told
- Break a big project into 3+ smaller tasks
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule (9+ hours)

## 🔍 College & Career Exploration

### What to Do This Year
- Take a "what interests you?" quiz online (with a parent) — just for fun!
- Talk to family members about their jobs — what do they like? What's hard?
- Watch age-appropriate career videos (YouTube channels about different jobs)
- Start noticing what subjects make you curious — math? Science? Writing? Art?
- Visit a local college campus if possible — just to see what it looks like!

### Conversation Starters
- "What jobs use [my favorite subject]?"
- "What did you want to be when you were my age?"
- "Can we visit a college campus this year just to walk around?"

## 💪 Personal Growth

### Fall Semester
- Practice introducing yourself and making eye contact
- Set one personal goal for the semester (improve at a sport, read more, learn a skill)
- Learn to handle disagreements respectfully — use "I feel..." statements
- Start managing your own morning routine independently

### Spring Semester
- Reflect on what you're proud of from this year
- Write down 3 things you want to try next year
- Practice asking for help when you need it — teachers, parents, peers
- Start learning about money basics — saving, budgeting your allowance

### Skills to Master
- Wake up and get ready for school without repeated reminders
- Speak up in class or group settings at least once per day
- Handle a setback without giving up (bad grade, lost game, etc.)
- Set and track a personal goal independently

## ☀️ Summer Checklist
- [ ] Read at least 3 books for fun
- [ ] Try one new activity (camp, sport, volunteering, or class)
- [ ] Practice a skill you want to improve (writing, math, an instrument)
- [ ] Visit a library, museum, or college campus
- [ ] Set 2-3 goals for 7th grade
- [ ] Organize your school supplies early

## ⚠️ Red Flags to Watch
- Regularly missing assignments or getting zeros
- Avoiding all extracurricular activities
- Spending all free time on screens with no balance
- Not knowing any teachers by name by end of first quarter
- Consistently staying up past 10 PM on school nights

## 🔗 How This Connects to College
You might think "I'm only in 6th grade — why does this matter?" Here's why: **every great college student started by building great habits.** The organization, time management, and curiosity you develop now become automatic by high school. You're not preparing for college applications — you're building the person who will crush those applications in a few years.

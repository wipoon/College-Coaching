# Dealing with Setbacks

## Setbacks Are Normal
Every single successful person has failed — multiple times. A bad grade, not making a team, losing a friend, struggling with a class — these aren't signs that you're failing at life. They're signs that you're trying, and trying is the only path to growth.

## Common Student Setbacks
- Failed a test or got a low grade
- Didn't make the team or get into a club
- Had a conflict with a friend or teacher
- Felt overwhelmed by homework or activities
- Compared yourself to someone and felt "not enough"
- Made a mistake you regret

## The RECOVER Framework
1. **R — Recognize** your feelings. It's okay to be upset, frustrated, or sad.
2. **E — Express** what happened. Talk to someone or write it down.
3. **C — Consider** what you can learn. What would you do differently?
4. **O — Own** your part. Take responsibility without beating yourself up.
5. **V — Visualize** moving forward. What does the next step look like?
6. **E — Execute** — Take one small action today toward recovery.
7. **R — Reflect** after a week. How do you feel now? What did you learn?

## Reframing Setbacks
| Instead of thinking... | Try thinking... |
|----------------------|----------------|
| "I'm a failure" | "I failed at one thing, and I can try again" |
| "Everyone else is better" | "I'm on my own timeline" |
| "I'll never get this" | "I haven't gotten this YET" |
| "This is unfair" | "What CAN I control in this situation?" |
| "I should give up" | "What's one small thing I can do right now?" |

## When to Ask for Help
- You've felt sad or anxious for more than two weeks
- A setback is affecting your sleep, eating, or friendships
- You feel unsafe or like hurting yourself → **Tell a trusted adult immediately**
- You've tried to fix it yourself and nothing is working
- You're avoiding school or activities because of stress

**Remember:** Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness. School counselors, parents, teachers, and other trusted adults are there for exactly these moments.

## Building Resilience
- Keep a "wins journal" — write down 1 thing that went well each day
- After a setback, make a plan within 24 hours (even a small one)
- Surround yourself with supportive people
- Remember past setbacks you overcame — you've done this before
- Practice self-compassion: treat yourself like you'd treat a good friend

# Growth Mindset — Your Superpower

## Fixed vs. Growth Mindset
- **Fixed mindset:** "I'm bad at math. I'll never get it." → Avoids challenges, gives up easily.
- **Growth mindset:** "I'm not great at math YET. I can improve with practice." → Embraces challenges, learns from mistakes.

Your brain is like a muscle — the more you work it, the stronger it gets. Every time you struggle with something and keep trying, your brain literally grows new connections. **Struggle is not failure — it's growth.**

## How to Build a Growth Mindset
1. **Replace "I can't" with "I can't YET"** — Add "yet" to any negative self-talk
2. **Celebrate effort, not just results** — "I studied hard" matters as much as "I got an A"
3. **Learn from mistakes** — After a bad grade, ask "What can I do differently?" not "Why am I so dumb?"
4. **Embrace challenges** — If something feels hard, that means you're learning
5. **Stop comparing** — Your journey is YOUR journey. Someone else's success doesn't diminish yours.

## Growth Mindset in Action
- **Bad grade on a test?** → "What questions did I miss? How will I study differently next time?"
- **Didn't make the team?** → "What skills can I work on? Is there another activity where I can grow?"
- **Friend is doing better?** → "Good for them! What can I learn from how they approach things?"
- **Homework feels impossible?** → "I'll try for 15 minutes. If I'm still stuck, I'll ask for help."

## Famous Growth Mindset Examples
- Michael Jordan was cut from his high school basketball team
- J.K. Rowling was rejected by 12 publishers before Harry Potter
- Albert Einstein didn't speak until age 4 and was told he'd "never amount to much"
- Every successful person has a story of failure before success

## Daily Practice
- Each night, write down one thing you struggled with and what you learned from it
- Each morning, tell yourself one positive thing about the day ahead
- Each week, try one thing that's slightly outside your comfort zone

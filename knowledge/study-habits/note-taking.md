# Note-Taking Strategies

## Why Good Notes Matter
Students who take effective notes score 34% higher on tests. Your notes are your personal study guide — they should capture what YOU need to remember, not every word the teacher says.

## Methods

### The Cornell Method (Best for middle and high school)
Divide your page into 3 sections:
1. **Right column (Notes):** Write main ideas and details during class
2. **Left column (Cues):** After class, write questions or keywords that match your notes
3. **Bottom (Summary):** Write a 2-3 sentence summary of the whole page

### Mind Mapping (Great for visual learners)
- Put the main topic in the center of your page
- Branch out with subtopics, details, and connections
- Use colors, symbols, and drawings
- Best for: brainstorming, reviewing big concepts, connecting ideas

### Outline Method (Best for organized, linear topics)
- Use Roman numerals (I, II, III) for main topics
- Capital letters (A, B, C) for subtopics
- Numbers (1, 2, 3) for details
- Best for: history, science, structured lectures

### The Charting Method (Best for comparison topics)
- Create a table with columns for different categories
- Fill in rows as you learn new information
- Best for: comparing historical events, scientific processes, literary characters

## Tips for Better Notes
- **Don't write everything** — Listen for key phrases: "This is important," "Remember this," "The main idea is..."
- **Use abbreviations** — Create your own shorthand (w/ = with, bc = because, → = leads to)
- **Leave space** — Skip lines between topics so you can add details later
- **Review within 24 hours** — Re-read your notes the same day. Fill in gaps while your memory is fresh.
- **Use color** — Highlight key terms, underline definitions, star important concepts
- **Date every page** — You'll thank yourself when studying for finals

## Digital vs. Handwritten
Research shows **handwriting notes leads to better learning** because it forces you to process and summarize rather than transcribe. However, typed notes are better for speed and organization. **Recommendation:** Handwrite in class, type up important notes for review.

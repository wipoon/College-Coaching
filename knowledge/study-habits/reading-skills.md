# Building a Reading Habit

## Why Reading Matters for College
Students who read regularly score higher on the SAT/ACT, write better essays, have larger vocabularies, and think more critically. Reading is the single most impactful habit for long-term academic success.

## Reading Goals by Grade
- **6th Grade:** 12+ books per year (1 per month), 20 minutes daily
- **7th Grade:** 15-20 books per year, 25 minutes daily
- **8th Grade:** 15-20 books per year, 30 minutes daily, include nonfiction
- **9th-10th Grade:** 12-15 books per year, 30+ minutes daily, diverse genres
- **11th-12th Grade:** 10-12 books per year, include college-level texts

## How to Build the Habit
1. **Start small** — Even 10 minutes before bed is a start
2. **Choose books YOU enjoy** — Nobody said it has to be "classic literature"
3. **Always have a book with you** — Physical or on your phone/tablet
4. **Set a specific reading time** — Before bed, during lunch, after school
5. **Track your progress** — Keep a list of books you've read (it feels great to see it grow!)

## What to Read
- **Fiction:** Builds empathy, vocabulary, and imagination
- **Nonfiction:** Builds knowledge, critical thinking, and argument analysis
- **News/Articles:** Builds current events awareness and reading speed
- **Mix genres:** Fantasy, science fiction, biography, history, science, memoir

## Tips for Reluctant Readers
- Try audiobooks while walking or exercising — they count!
- Start with graphic novels or short story collections
- Find books based on movies or shows you already love
- Join a book club or reading challenge for motivation
- Ask friends, teachers, or librarians for recommendations
- No shame in putting down a book you don't enjoy — try another one!

## For Parents
- Model reading — let your child see you read
- Don't police what they read (within reason) — any reading builds skills
- Visit bookstores and libraries together
- Discuss what you're both reading at dinner
- Gift books based on their interests

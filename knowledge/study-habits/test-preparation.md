# Test Preparation Strategies

## The Science of Studying
Your brain learns through **retrieval practice** (testing yourself) and **spaced repetition** (reviewing over time). Just re-reading notes is one of the LEAST effective study methods. Here's what actually works:

## Top Study Techniques

### 1. Active Recall (Test Yourself!)
- Close your notes and try to write down everything you remember
- Use flashcards — but don't just flip and read. Try to answer BEFORE flipping
- Teach the material to someone else (or pretend to teach an empty chair)
- Take practice tests whenever available
- **Why it works:** Forcing your brain to retrieve information strengthens the memory

### 2. Spaced Repetition
- Don't cram the night before! Spread studying over multiple days
- **Day 1:** Learn the material
- **Day 2:** Review for 15 minutes
- **Day 4:** Review for 10 minutes
- **Day 7:** Quick review before the test
- **Why it works:** Each review strengthens the memory path in your brain

### 3. The Feynman Technique
1. Write the topic at the top of a blank page
2. Explain it in simple words as if teaching a 5-year-old
3. Identify gaps — where did you get stuck or vague?
4. Go back to your notes and fill those gaps
5. Simplify again until you can explain it clearly
- **Why it works:** If you can't explain it simply, you don't truly understand it

### 4. Practice Problems
- For math and science: do MORE problems than assigned
- Don't just read solved examples — cover the solution and try yourself first
- Focus on problems you get wrong — that's where learning happens
- Create your own practice problems

## Test Preparation Timeline
- **1 week before:** Review all notes, make a study guide, identify weak areas
- **4-5 days before:** Focus on weak areas, make flashcards, do practice problems
- **2-3 days before:** Practice tests, teach concepts to others, review flashcards
- **Night before:** Light review only (30 min max), pack everything, get 8+ hours of sleep
- **Test morning:** Eat breakfast, review flashcards briefly, arrive early, stay calm

## Managing Test Anxiety
- **Prepare well** — Most anxiety comes from feeling unprepared
- **Deep breathing** — 4 counts in, hold 4, out 4. Do this 3 times before the test
- **Positive self-talk** — Replace "I'm going to fail" with "I prepared and I'll do my best"
- **Read all questions first** — Start with ones you know to build confidence
- **Skip and return** — Don't get stuck on hard questions. Mark them and come back

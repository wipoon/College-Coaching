# Career Exploration Activities

## Why Start Early?
You don't need to know what you want to be. But exploring careers early helps you choose better classes, find activities you love, and discover strengths you didn't know you had.

## Activities by Grade Level

### Middle School (6th-8th Grade)
- **Interest inventories** — Take free quizzes online to explore career categories
- **Family interviews** — Ask 5 adults about their jobs: What do they do? What do they like? How did they get there?
- **Career day** — Attend school career days and ask questions
- **Job shadowing** — Spend a half-day watching someone work (with parent help to arrange)
- **Explore hobbies** — Your future career might start as a hobby (coding, writing, building, art)
- **Read biographies** — Learn how people in different fields got their start

### Early High School (9th-10th Grade)
- **Career aptitude tests** — Take more detailed assessments (many schools offer these free)
- **Informational interviews** — Email or call professionals to ask about their careers (great writing practice!)
- **Volunteering** — Volunteer in fields that interest you (hospital, animal shelter, library, tech nonprofit)
- **Summer programs** — Look for career exploration camps and summer enrichment programs
- **Course alignment** — Choose high school classes that connect to potential career interests
- **Research industries** — Start understanding what different sectors pay, require, and look like day-to-day

### Late High School (11th-12th Grade)
- **Internships** — Even short ones give real-world experience and look great on applications
- **College major research** — Connect career interests to specific college programs
- **Professional networking** — Attend career events, connect on LinkedIn (with parent guidance)
- **Portfolio building** — Start documenting your work, projects, and accomplishments
- **Gap year research** — Explore meaningful gap year options if college isn't the immediate next step

## Questions to Ask Yourself
- What subjects make me lose track of time?
- What problems in the world do I want to help solve?
- Do I prefer working with people, data, things, or ideas?
- Do I like structure or flexibility in my day?
- What would I do for free because I love it?

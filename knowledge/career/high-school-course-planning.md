# High School Course Planning

## Why Course Selection Matters
Colleges look at WHAT classes you took, not just your grades. A student with a B+ in AP Chemistry shows more than a student with an A in regular Science. Choose the most rigorous courses you can handle while maintaining strong grades.

## Core Requirements (Typical College-Prep Track)
| Subject | Years | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| English | 4 | Every year of high school |
| Math | 3-4 | Through Algebra II minimum; Pre-Calc or Calculus preferred |
| Science | 3-4 | Biology, Chemistry, Physics recommended |
| Social Studies | 3-4 | Including US History, World History, Government |
| Foreign Language | 2-3 | Same language; 3-4 years for selective colleges |
| Electives | Varies | Arts, technology, AP courses, career-focused |

## Course Planning by Grade

### 8th Grade (Planning Year)
- Meet with your school counselor to discuss high school course selection
- Take placement tests if offered (math, language)
- Consider honors track for your strongest subjects
- Ask about AP or IB programs at your high school

### 9th Grade
- Core: English 9, Algebra I or Geometry, Biology, World History, Foreign Language
- Consider: 1 honors class in your strongest subject
- Elective: Something that interests you (art, coding, music, etc.)

### 10th Grade
- Core: English 10, Geometry or Algebra II, Chemistry, US History, Foreign Language
- Consider: 1-2 AP courses (AP World History, AP Human Geography are good starters)
- Take the PSAT for practice

### 11th Grade
- Core: English 11 or AP Language, Pre-Calculus or AP Calculus, Physics, AP US History
- Push for: 2-3 AP courses aligned with your interests and strengths
- Electives aligned with potential major/career

### 12th Grade
- Continue rigorous courses — colleges CHECK senior year schedule
- AP courses in areas of interest/strength
- Senior capstone or independent study if available
- Don't coast — admissions officers notice

## Tips
- **Balance challenge and success** — Don't take 6 AP courses if it'll tank your GPA and health
- **Align with interests** — Love science? Take AP Bio AND AP Chem. Love writing? AP Lang AND AP Lit.
- **Talk to older students** — They know which teachers and classes are most valuable
- **Think about college applications** — Selective schools want to see you challenged yourself
- **Keep options open** — Take broad courses early, specialize later

# Grade Translator

Your school uses a 0–100% grading system. Many college prep resources, roadmaps, and admissions guides refer to letter grades (A, B+, etc.). Use this chart to translate between the two.

## Standard Letter Grade to Percentage Conversion

| Letter Grade | Percentage | What It Means |
|:---:|:---:|:---|
| A+ | 97–100% | Exceptional — top of the class |
| A  | 93–96%  | Excellent — strong mastery |
| A- | 90–92%  | Very good — minor gaps |
| B+ | 87–89%  | Good — above solid |
| B  | 83–86%  | Solid — meets expectations well |
| B- | 80–82%  | Decent — some room to grow |
| C+ | 77–79%  | Fair — adequate understanding |
| C  | 73–76%  | Average — basic competency |
| C- | 70–72%  | Below average — needs improvement |
| D+ | 67–69%  | Poor — at risk |
| D  | 63–66%  | Very poor — barely passing |
| D- | 60–62%  | Minimal pass |
| F  | Below 60% | Failing |

## Quick Translation for Common Roadmap Goals

When our roadmap says...    →    Aim for this percentage:

- **"Maintain a B+ or higher"** → Keep your grades at **87% or above**
- **"Aim for straight A's"** → Target **93%+ in every class**
- **"GPA of 3.5+"** → Average around **90% across all classes**
- **"GPA of 3.0+"** → Average around **83% across all classes**
- **"No grades below a C"** → Keep every class at **73% or above**
- **"Honor Roll"** → Typically **90%+ average** (check your school)
- **"High Honor Roll"** → Typically **93%+ average** (check your school)

## Why This Matters for College

- **Competitive colleges** (Ivy League, top 50) typically expect a GPA of 3.7+ (about **90%+ average**)
- **State universities** generally look for 3.0+ (about **83%+ average**)
- **Community colleges** accept most students regardless of GPA
- Colleges recalculate your GPA using their own scale, so **consistent high percentages matter more than any single letter grade**
